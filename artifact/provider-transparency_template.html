Provider disclosure audit

Nobody gets an A.

Ten inference providers, graded on a single question: if a researcher wanted to know what actually served their result — and whether it has changed since — could they find out from the vendor's own disclosures? Four criteria, two points each. The best score in the sweep is 6 out of 8.

The four criteria

Each is scored absent (0), partial (1), or solid (2) against what the audit found on the provider's own pages. Every score carries its reason — open a row to read it next to the evidence it came from.

The ledger

Verified against a live page snapshot. Click any provider to open its dossier — the verbatim quotations, what the audit recorded, and the reasoning behind each of its four scores.

Ungraded leads

Surfaced by one pass of search agents and confirmed by nobody. They carry no quotations, and they get no grade — scoring an unverified report would launder a search result into a finding. These are a map of where to look next, in rough order of how much confirming them would change the picture.

Source: findings/provider_transparency.json (the audit) joined with findings/provider_grades.json (the scores) in the openrouter-reliable-research-search project. Every quotation was located verbatim in a snapshot of the cited page taken by scripts/fetch_provider_transparency.py; a quote that can no longer be found on the live page fails the test suite rather than standing.

Letters are computed from the scores at build time, never stored — a grade cannot drift from its own reasoning. This is vendor testimony either way: the weakest evidence class the project recognises, which is why the practice it recommends is to record the endpoint yourself at run time rather than trust any of it.
